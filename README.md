# Sofia Baltazar Portfolio Website

This is the personal portfolio website of Sofia Baltazar, a Creative Technologist, Designer, and Systems & Project Specialist. 
The site showcases services, portfolio projects, lead capture forms, and client engagement tools.

## Purpose and Client Positioning

This website is designed to position Sofia Baltazar as a skilled, approachable, and results-driven freelancer. From a client’s perspective, the site communicates:

- **Expertise:** Showcases completed projects with detailed case studies.
- **Professionalism:** Includes organized forms, legal pages, and clear service offerings.
- **Clarity:** The site focuses on Sofia Baltazar’s personal brand, without confusing business names.
- **Action-Oriented:** Guides clients to engage through forms, bookings, or contact requests.

## Website Features

- Homepage: Introduces Sofia Baltazar and key offerings.
- About Me: Professional background and skills.
- Projects: Detailed project case studies with problem, solution, results, and tools.
- Forms: Book a call, freelancer inquiry, free guides.
- Legal: Terms, Privacy Policy, and Legal disclaimers.
- Freebies: Offers downloadable resources.

## Tech Stack

- HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript
- Responsive design for desktop and mobile
- Hosted on GitHub Pages
- Connected to Hubspot

## HubSpot Integration

This website is connected to **HubSpot CRM**, allowing all forms and lead capture mechanisms to automatically send data to HubSpot. This ensures:

- Leads are collected efficiently and stored securely.
- Clients can be followed up systematically.
- Workflow automation can be implemented for onboarding or nurturing.
- Website interactions and form submissions are tracked in one central system.

Integration enhances the **professionalism and reliability** of Sofia Baltazar’s freelance services, showing clients that systems are structured and results-focused.

## Brand Alignment

- The website fully represents the personal brand of Sofia Baltazar.
- Previous mentions of "Little Things" and "Master Business Systems" have been removed to maintain clarity and consistency.
- Focus is entirely on the freelancer identity and portfolio services.
